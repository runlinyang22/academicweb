Welcome! I study how artificial intelligence and technological change reorganize geographical space.

My research examines urban-regional transformation, spatial inequality and the changing relationship between technology, mobility and territorial organization. I am particularly interested in whether AI-mediated forms of coordination can alter established patterns of metropolitan concentration and create new possibilities for regional equity.

Through theoretical development and empirical spatial analysis, my work addresses the future of human geography in an era when intelligent technologies increasingly participate in the production and governance of space.

Research themes

AI-mediated spatial transformation

How intelligent technologies, remote connectivity and digital coordination reshape urban-regional structures and mobility.

Spatial inequality and regional repair

How changes in accessibility and opportunity may narrow, reproduce or redirect territorial disparities.

Space–Scene–Scenario framework

A human-geographical framework integrating spatial structure, socio-technical settings and possible futures.
